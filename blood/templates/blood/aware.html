What is Thalassemia?

Thalassemia is an inherited blood disorder that causes the body to produce an abnormal form or inadequate amount of hemoglobin. This leads to excessive destruction of red blood cells, which causes anemia. It can be mild or severe and may require frequent blood transfusions.

It is passed from parents to children and is more common in certain parts of the world, including South Asia.
